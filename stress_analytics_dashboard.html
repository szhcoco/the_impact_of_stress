Research Overview

Stress significantly impacts academic performance, but the relationship is complex and varies between individuals. This analysis examines physiological stress markers to understand their correlation with exam scores.

Heart Rate Variability

Cardiovascular response to stress and cognitive load during examinations

Electrodermal Activity

Skin conductance reflecting emotional arousal and stress responses

Body Temperature

Thermal regulation changes in response to psychological stress

Physical Movement

Fidgeting and movement patterns indicating restlessness and anxiety

Key Findings

Our analysis reveals complex relationships between physiological stress markers and academic performance

Individual Variation

Stress responses vary significantly between students, requiring personalized approaches

Optimal Stress Levels

Moderate stress can enhance performance, while high stress often impairs cognitive function

Temporal Patterns

Stress responses change throughout the exam period, with distinct pre-, during-, and post-test phases

Actionable Insights

Real-time monitoring can help develop targeted stress management strategies for improved outcomes
